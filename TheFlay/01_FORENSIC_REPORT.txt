SECTION 5 — FINDINGS & ANALYSIS
──────────────────────────────────

5.1  TELEMETRY EXFILTRATION (NST-STG-0001)
  The staging frontend sends a Google-Analytics-style pageview hit to an
  analytics-staging subdomain. The CID (client identifier) is transmitted with
  each hit. No consent banner or opt-out mechanism was observed in the captured
  traffic. Under FISMA and NIST SP 800-53 (control AU-3, SI-12), collection of
  tracking identifiers on federal staging systems requires explicit authorization.
  The cid field has been SHA-256 hashed in this report (see CID SHA-256 above).

5.2  CSP VIOLATIONS (NST-STG-0002)
  The CSP report endpoint received a violation for the directive 'script-src-elem'
  with blocked-uri: inline. This indicates either (a) the nonce is not being
  injected into the inline script at line 47 of the publications page, or
  (b) the nonce value is static/cached and reused across sessions, rendering it
  ineffective. The response body was truncated at capture (IsTruncated: true);
  additional violations may exist beyond the captured payload.

5.3  SENTRY PII EXPOSURE (NST-STG-0003)
  The Sentry SDK is transmitting user.id and user.email in the error envelope
  to o123456.ingest.sentry.io (external). The SDK's beforeSend hook has not
  been configured to strip PII. This constitutes a potential data breach of
  PII to a third-party processor not listed in the staging data-flow inventory.
  WAF rule firing was noted on the follow-on POST (blocked by WAF).

5.4  SERVICE WORKER INTERCEPT (NST-STG-0004)
  /sw.js v1.2.3 intercepts all requests to analytics-staging hostnames and
  passes them through with credentials:include, meaning session cookies are
  forwarded to the telemetry endpoint even from cached SW responses — bypassing
  the WAF for subsequent requests. WAF rule WAF-SW-001 was triggered on first
  registration.
